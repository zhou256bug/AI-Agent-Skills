# Speech Crafting for Chinese Executives (B6 Mode)

## Trigger

User asks for English speech/draft after cross-cultural analysis for a Chinese executive with limited English. Route table entry: "B6 话术撰写" in SKILL.md.

## Must-do before speech

Cross-cultural analysis FIRST (Hofstede dimensions + framework). Speech is the OUTPUT of cultural diagnosis, not a translation exercise. Never skip the analysis.

## Content signals — every speech must carry three:

1. **Posture signal** (PD adjustment — Chinese PD=80 is high; most European PD=35–55. Must de-hierarchize)
2. **Certainty signal** (UA matching — give concrete numbers, dates, decision power)
3. **Solidarity signal** (collectivist vs individualist — "I'm here to work WITH you")

## Language rules for non-fluent English speakers

- Max 15 words per sentence, prefer 8–12
- Use only top-1000 English words
- Avoid: strategy, leverage, synergy, optimize, ecosystem, paradigm, stakeholder
- Simple SVO structure, no nested clauses
- 1-2-3 enumeration ("Here's what I can do: One... Two... Three...")
- Open with posture, close with call-to-action + thanks

## Length

| Format | Duration | Word count |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Full speech | 1.5–2 min | 200–250 |
| Key points | 30 sec | 60–80 |
| One-liner | — | ≤25 |

## Delivery notes (third-party handoff)

When user says "give me English, I'll pass it on" — always append:

1. **Usage notes**: "全文大约1分半钟，前30秒最关键" / "Key moment is the first 30 seconds — set the right posture first"
2. **3 keyword anchors**: the 3 words the executive only needs to remember
3. **Chinese cue card** (optional): short Chinese version for the executive to glance at during the speech

## Pitfalls

1. No sentences over 20 words — non-native speakers stumble
2. No metaphors/idioms — "don't put all eggs in one basket" fails cross-culturally
3. No more than 2 minutes — non-fluent speakers need rehearsal, keep it short
4. Don't translate Chinese → English directly — re-express for the target culture's PD/UA
5. Don't write a script to memorize — give framework + keywords, not a verbatim script
